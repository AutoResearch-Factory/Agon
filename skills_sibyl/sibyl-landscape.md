# Research Landscape

## Novelty Check

### Collision Classification
exact_match: core idea already published — drop or substantially revise. partial_overlap: similar approach with meaningful differences — document what is different. related_work: prior art that should be cited but does not undermine novelty.

### Novelty Scoring
9-10: Genuinely novel, no close prior work found. 7-8: Novel with minor overlap, differences clear and defensible. 5-6: Partial overlap, needs repositioning to claim novelty. 3-4: Substantial overlap, core contribution is weak. 1-2: Already done, drop or radically change.

### Anti-Patterns
Do NOT rubber-stamp novelty without searching. Do NOT dismiss an idea for vaguely related work — be precise about what overlaps. Do NOT conflate "related work" with "already done." Do NOT skip candidates — every candidate must be assessed.

## Literature Survey

### Survey Principles
Speed first: 5-10 papers per direction sufficient — do not aim for exhaustive coverage. Quality filtering: only record directly relevant papers, filter out noise. Dual-source: arXiv for cutting-edge paper details, Web search for overall trends and resources.

### Survey Output
Field overview (current state, dominant paradigms), core references with limitations, SOTA methods and benchmarks, identified research gaps, available resources (code, datasets, pretrained models), implications for idea generation (which directions worth exploring, which saturated).

### Implementation Strategy
Adopt: use existing implementation directly (high match, well-maintained, license-compatible). Extend: fork or wrap, modify to fit needs. Compose: combine 2-3 small tools to build functionality. Build: implement from scratch, reference design patterns from survey.
