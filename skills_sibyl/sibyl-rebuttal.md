# Rebuttal

Nine-agent collaborative rebuttal: decompose → strategize → evidence → write → QA → synthesize.

## Concern Decomposition

Atomic per concern: one issue per entry. Classify: weakness / question / suggestion / minor / factual_error. Severity: critical / major / minor. Requires: argument_only / evidence / experiment / revision.

## Response Strategy

Four strategies: direct_answer (we have evidence), acknowledge_and_plan (valid, propose future work), respectful_disagree (reviewer misunderstood, clarify), concede_and_mitigate (valid weakness, acknowledge and bound impact). Prioritize: severity > frequency (multiple reviewers) > addressability > score impact.

## Evidence

Scholarly: NEVER fabricate citations — every reference real and verifiable. Full citation with venue and year. Prioritize recent top-venue papers. Supporting citations, theoretical backing, empirical corroboration, counterexamples to reviewer claims. If no supporting evidence exists, state honestly.

Theoretical: strengthen proofs (tighten bounds, add missing steps, clarify assumptions), connect to established frameworks. If concern is valid, bound its impact honestly.

Experiments: feasible within rebuttal timeline. Prefer quick decisive experiments over comprehensive. Design for clear positive OR negative signals. Each: objective, design, expected outcome, effort, priority, fallback if negative.

## Writing

Tone: professional, respectful. Thank for valid criticism. Never dismissive. Confident but not arrogant — back claims with evidence. Strategic concessions strengthen overall rebuttal. Lead with answer, then evidence. Quote specific concern before responding. Concrete numbers where available. Acknowledge limitations where genuine.

Structure per reviewer: thank → concern-by-concern (quote → response → evidence). Open with summary of what was addressed. Close with confidence in strengthened contribution.

## Creative Advocacy

Find angles others miss: alternative interpretations, broader context where method shines, hidden strengths, cross-field analogies. Passionate but NEVER dishonest.

## QA

Logic consistency (contradictions between team members or with original paper?), coverage completeness (all critical+major addressed? vague "we will investigate"?), evidence verification (citations real and correctly attributed?), tone audit (defensive or dismissive language?), cross-round consistency (no argument drift without acknowledgment?).
