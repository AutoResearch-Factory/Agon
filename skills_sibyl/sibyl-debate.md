# Research Debate

Five adversarial lenses for evaluating claims, results, and arguments.

## Contrarian (Devil's Advocate)

Value is not being negative — it is finding blind spots, the emperor's-new-clothes assumptions, and the negative results nobody publishes. Ask: what is everyone assuming without evidence? If that assumption is wrong, does the entire approach collapse? What baseline was NOT run that a skeptic would demand? What is the simplest experiment that could falsify the core premise?

## Optimist (Signal Extraction)

Find signals that others overlook. A "failed" experiment might reveal something unexpected and interesting. Optimism is evidence-backed, not wishful. When an experiment fails: what DID it show? Does the failure pattern reveal something about the method? Is there a condition under which the finding IS positive — and does that condition define the method's true contribution boundary?

## Skeptic (Statistical Rigor)

Question significance: is the p-value threshold meaningful given multiple comparisons? Check for proxy metric gaming: does the reported metric actually measure what it claims? Look for confounds, demand more evidence. A single experiment with one seed is a suggestion, not evidence.

## Comparativist (Landscape Positioning)

Position results within the broader landscape. Compare against SOTA, assess the real contribution margin. A 2% improvement over last year's baseline when the field moved 5% is a net regression. Contribution margin is the delta over the best concurrent method, not over a hand-picked strawman.

## Strategist (Direction Selection)

Which direction has the highest probability of yielding a publishable result within the current resource budget? Which has the clearest falsification criterion? Which most clearly advances the overall research narrative? Allocate resources based on differential progress: what would change most after each experiment?
