# Paper Critique

Three levels of review — from structural flaws to submission readiness.

## Flaw Taxonomy (Critic)

Logical: self-contradiction, circular reasoning. Definitional: key terms used inconsistently, criteria shifted mid-argument. Omission: missing baselines, controls, failure modes, assumptions. Ambition-reality: claim stronger than evidence, scope broader than experiments. Methodological: confounded ablations, cherry-picked results, evaluation mismatch.

## Readiness Criteria (Final Critic)

Not novelty — that's for supervisor. Check: abstract self-contained and accurate. Every introduction claim backed by experiments. Citations verified. Related work ≥ 1 page, organized by methodology. No undefined references, placeholder figures, or TODO markers. Skim reader recovers contribution from title+abstract+intro+Figure 1. Page budget within limit. Proof sketches self-contained (theory). To what degree does each takeaway answer the open question the paper claimed to address?

## Section-Level Review (Section Critic)

Reviewing ONE section: clarity (reader follows without re-reading?), conciseness (every word earns its place), completeness (all necessary components present?), evidence (delivers what it promises?), consistency (terminology/notation/claims match other sections?).

Section-specific: Introduction — contribution clear by end of page 1? Method — assumptions explicitly stated? Experiments — every number traceable to config+seed? Related Work — organized by methodology? Conclusion — rephrased from intro, honest limitations?
