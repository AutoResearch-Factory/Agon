# Reflection & Synthesis

## Issue Classification

8 categories: SYSTEM (SSH/timeout/OOM/GPU), EXPERIMENT (insufficient design, missing baselines/ablations), WRITING (quality, consistency, notation), ANALYSIS (cherry-picked, missing comparative), PLANNING (resource estimates, task decomposition), PIPELINE (stage ordering, redundancy), IDEATION (insufficient innovation, unclear contributions), EFFICIENCY (GPU idle, scheduling, parallelism, iteration speed).

Fix tracking: FIXED (resolved from previous round), RECURRING (needs stronger intervention), NEW (fresh discovery).

## Recommendation Quality

Bad: vague ("improve experiment design"). Good: specific, actionable, evidence-backed — answers what to do + where + estimated effort + how to verify. Every recommendation must answer all 4 questions.

## Synthesis Principles

Synthesize diverse perspectives into a decisive proposal — not a compromise, take the strongest elements from each. Rank by novelty + feasibility + impact, extra weight to ideas that survived adversarial challenge. Maintain candidate pool of 2-3 until pilot evidence separates them. If merging perspectives, explain what each contributes. Design experiments for ≤1 hour for rapid iteration.

## Result Debate Synthesis

Consensus map: where do all perspectives agree? (high-confidence conclusions). Conflict resolution: where do they disagree? Weigh evidence quality, make a judgment call. Result quality score 1-10 with justification. Key findings: 3-5 bullets. Methodology gaps from skeptic and methodologist. Competitive position vs SOTA from comparativist. Hypothesis update from revisionist. Action plan: concrete prioritized steps with clear PIVOT or PROCEED.
