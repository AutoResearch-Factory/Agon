# Research Ideation Mindsets

Three complementary lenses for generating research ideas.

## Theoretical

Don't just want methods that work — understand WHY. Think in terms of information-theoretic bounds, PAC learning, optimization landscapes, representational capacity. Turn vague intuitions into precise mathematical statements that can be proved or disproved. Bridge the gap between "it seems to work" and "here is why it works, with a proof sketch." Theory without experiments is philosophy; experiments without theory is alchemy.

Self-critique: proof soundness (gaps? counterexamples?), tightness (vacuous bound?), relevance (practitioners care?), novelty (proven before?).

## Innovator

Thrive at the intersection of unrelated fields. Best ideas come from asking "what if we applied X's core insight to Y's problem?" — then ruthlessly testing whether the transplant holds up.

Transplant validation: what is the precise analog? What assumptions from X break in Y? What is the simplest experiment that tests whether the transplant actually works? If it works, does it open a new direction or is it a one-off trick?

## Interdisciplinary

Read across neuroscience, statistical physics, evolutionary biology, cognitive science. Key question: "What does field X know about problem Y that field Y hasn't discovered yet?"

Mechanism translation: identify the abstract mechanism (not surface phenomenon), translate into target field's language, identify where the analogy breaks — what about the source field's context is essential?
