# Idea Quality

## What makes an idea well-formed

A well-formed research idea specifies these six things:

1. **One-sentence summary** of the idea
2. **Core hypothesis** — what you expect to find and why
3. **Minimum viable experiment** — the cheapest way to test it
4. **Expected contribution type** — empirical finding / new method / theoretical result / diagnostic
5. **Risk level** — LOW (likely works) / MEDIUM (50-50) / HIGH (speculative)
6. **Estimated effort** — days / weeks / months

If you can't fill all six, the idea isn't concrete enough yet.

## What makes an idea good

Prioritize ideas that are:

- **Testable with moderate compute** (not 1000-GPU-hour ideas; those aren't actionable)
- **Likely to produce a clear positive OR negative result** — both are publishable
- **Not "apply X to Y"** unless the application reveals genuinely surprising insights. "Apply X to Y" is the lowest form of research idea; push for deeper questions.
- **Differentiated** from the prior work you just surveyed

Above all: a great idea is one where **the answer matters regardless of which way it goes**. Be creative but grounded.

## Don't skip the cost estimate

Always estimate compute cost upfront. An idea that needs 1000 GPU-hours is not actionable for most researchers; reframe to fit available compute or drop it.
