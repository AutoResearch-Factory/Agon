# Empirical Pilot

## Why pilot

Pilots are the key differentiator from paper-only validation — they give empirical signal. An idea with a positive pilot outranks a "sounds great" idea without evidence: empirical signal > theoretical appeal at this stage.

## Before launching

- **Estimate GPU-hours BEFORE launching.** If the estimate exceeds the per-pilot budget, reduce scale (fewer epochs, smaller subset) or flag the idea as "needs manual pilot". Do not just run it and hope.
- **Define the clear success metric upfront**, before the pilot runs. Defining it after results = cherry-pick.

## Reading the results

- When comparing pilots, **surprising findings deserve special attention** — they may signal a pivot, not just noise.
- Strong empirical signal **jumps** a theoretically appealing but untested idea ahead in the ranking.
- Document null and negative results as eliminated ideas — they're cheap kill signals that save future-you from re-discovering the same dead end.
