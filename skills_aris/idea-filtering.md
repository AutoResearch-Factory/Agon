# Idea Filtering

## Three-axis feasibility

For each candidate idea, evaluate feasibility on three axes:

- **Compute**: can we afford the GPU-hours?
- **Data**: is the dataset available, or do we have to build it?
- **Implementation**: is the engineering complexity within budget?

Eliminate any idea where any axis is prohibitive.

## The "So what?" test

If the experiment succeeds, does it change how people think? Or is the finding only "interesting" — true but not actionable?

A good research result is **actionable**, not just interesting. If a senior reviewer would shrug at the success case, kill the idea.

## Quantity first, quality second

Generate broadly, then filter ruthlessly. A pool of 8-12 candidates filtered to 4-6 survivors filtered to 2-3 piloted ideas is a normal funnel. Over-filtering early starves the funnel; under-filtering late wastes pilot budget.

## Devil's advocate (per surviving idea)

Ask four questions of each idea that survives the first-pass filter:

- What's the **strongest objection** a reviewer would raise?
- What's the **most likely failure mode** during execution?
- How would you **rank these for a top venue submission**?
- Of these, **which 2-3 would you actually work on**?

The last question is the harshest filter — many ideas survive logical critique but fail "would I personally bet weeks of work on this?"

## Be willing to kill

- Don't fall in love with any idea before validating it.
- Kill ideas early. Better to kill 10 bad ideas at filtering than to implement one and fail.
- Eliminated ideas aren't waste — document them as dead ends to save future-you the same path.
